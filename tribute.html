Dr.APJ Abdul Kalam

The missile man of India

Dr.Kalam wears a colonel's ribbon during the nuclear tests in 1998 and later on becomes the supreme commander of the armed forces.

Here's a time line of Dr.Kalam's life:

  • 1931- Born on 15^th october to a tamil muslim family in Rameswaram,Tamil Nadu
  • 1955- Moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1960- Joined the Aeronautical Developement Establishment of the Defence Research and Developement Organisation as a scientist
  • 1963-64- Visited NASA's Langley Research Centerin Hampton,Virginia;Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt,Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility
  • 1969- Moved to Indian Space Research Organization and helms India's first Satellite Launching Vehicle(SLV-III)
  • 1980- Leads India's entry in the space club by putting Rohini Satellite in the Earth's orbit
  • 1980-90- Develops Prithvi and Agni missiles
  • 1981- Receives the Padma Bhushan for his contribution to the field of science and technology
  • 1990- Honoured with india's second highest civilian honour,the Padma Vibhushan
  • 1992-99- Appointed India's chief scientific Adviser
  • 1997- Honoured with the Bharat Ratna,India's highest civilian honour for his contribution to the field of scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India
  • 1998- spearheads the Pokharan mission
  • 1991-2001- Serves as principal scientific adviser to the government of India
  • 2002-2007- Elected as India's 11th President
  • 2012- Launches "what can i give movement",a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption
  • 2015- Passes away on 27^th July in Shillong,Meghalaya while delivering a lecture at IIM,Shillong

A Scientist and statesman, kalam rose from humble beginnings to become one of India's most accomplished leader
